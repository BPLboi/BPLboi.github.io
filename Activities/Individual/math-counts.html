MathCounts

Overview

MathCounts is a mathematics competition for middle schoolers, that has local, state, and national-level events. The questions asked focus on branches of math slightly outside of the usual middle-school curriculum, such as combinatorics, so participating in the competition is a great way to learn some more mathematics!

At my middle school, there was a MathCounts club. Though in name it was dedicated to preparing for the MathCounts competition, the MathCounts club was closer to a vehicle through which students could learn math at an accelerated pace, and interact with others who also loved the subject.

Coaching

Since I had benefited a lot from the MathCounts club in middle school and thought it was a great way to introduce kids to mathematics, in 9th, 10th, and 11th grade I returned to it as an assistant coach.

In this role, I helped create handouts and homework assignments that would walk students through new mathematical ideas (including counting techniques such as "stars and bars", modular arithmetic, ways to analyze geometric figures, and more). After each handout, I would also provide detailed feedback on students' solutions to help them use them better.

I would also attend the team's weekly practices, where I helped grade the practice competitions that students took, and helped them with figuring out solutions to these questions after each practice.

Competing

In 6th, 7th, and 8th grade, I very much enjoyed participating in my middle school's MathCounts club.
Through it, I received my first introduction to competition mathematics, and learned the basics of combinatorics, algebra, number theory, and geometry.

At the club's weekly practices, we would do practice competitions, and then review them. Some older students in high school would help us with with solving the particularly challenging questions during the review period.

In addition, we had occasionally had homework sets, which were especially fun! Many of the questions challenged me, and I fondly remember spending large parts of my Thanksgiving break engrossed with them.

In 6th grade, after months of practice I ended up being one spot away from being among the ten students sent to the local competition.
In 7th grade, I significantly improved my mathematical skills, and ended up being among the top four individuals at my school. So, I went to the local competition, and succeeded to the state competition where I was 20th in the state.
In 8th grade, I continued learning more math, and again went to the state competition, where I had the 5th highest Sprint + Target round score. This qualified me for the countdown round, where I also placed 5th.

Though the club primarily focused on preparing for the MathCounts competition, I attended the Exeter Math Club Competition (EMCC) and Lexington Math Tournament (LMT) with teammates from the club.
